# Advanced Telecommunications Laboratory Experiments (Exp 11–20)

This repository contains detailed laboratory reports for **Experiments 11–20** in the Advanced Telecommunications Laboratory course. The experiments are conducted using the **Emona Telecoms-Trainer 101** and a **dual-channel 20 MHz oscilloscope**, covering topics in digital communications, signal processing, and modulation techniques.

---

---

## Learning Outcomes

By completing these experiments, students will:

- Master **sampling and reconstruction** techniques, understanding natural and sample-and-hold sampling, aliasing, and VCO timing.  
- Understand **PCM encoding and decoding**, quantization, and signal reconstruction for digital communication.  
- Observe the effects of **bandwidth limitation** on digital signals and learn signal restoration techniques.  
- Generate and analyze **digital modulation schemes**, including ASK, FSK, BPSK, and QPSK.  
- Explore **DSSS modulation/demodulation**, speech transmission, and interference (jamming) effects.  
- Apply **undersampling and SDR techniques** for DSBSC signal recovery and baseband observation.  
- Develop practical skills in **oscilloscope usage, waveform observation, and module interconnections** in analog and digital communications labs.
